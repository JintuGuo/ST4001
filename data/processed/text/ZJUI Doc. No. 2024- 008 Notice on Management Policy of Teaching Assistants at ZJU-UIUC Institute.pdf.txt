Notice on Management Policy of Teaching Assistants  
at ZJU-UIUC Institute 
 
1. General Information 
To promote educational reform, effectively ensure the quality of course 
instruction, further strengthen the students' abilities in knowledge 
application, communication, teaching practice and management, and 
enhance the overall quality of student cultivation in ZJU-UIUC Institute 
(hereinafter referred to as "ZJUI"), this policy is formulated in the light of 
the actual circumstances at ZJUI and pursuant to MOE document 
(Opinions of the Ministry of Education on Accelerating the Construction 
of High-level Undergraduate Education and Comprehensive Improvement 
of Training Capacity on Talents [2018] No.2)," Management Measures of 
Teaching Assistant by Graduate Students at Zhejiang University " (ZJU Fa 
[2019] No. 39), and other relevant documents. 
2. The Requirements and Responsibilities of Teaching Assistants 
(TA) 
2.1 Teaching assistants are an important supporting force in the academic 

- 2 - 
 
teaching of ZJUI. The main purpose of setting up TA positions is to help 
students and to improve their learning effectiveness. 
2.2 The responsibilities of TA include, but not limited to, attending classes, 
organizing 
seminars 
or 
discussion/tutorial 
sessions, 
classroom 
management or guidance, offering consultation in office hours, grading 
assignments or lab reports, participating in teaching preparation and 
assessment, developing/maintaining course website/platforms, collecting 
and analyzing course information, and other various supportive work. 
2.3 TAs are required to fulfill their duties, to accept guidance and 
supervision from the course instructor, adhere to relevant work 
arrangements, and complete their responsibilities with high quality and 
quantity. For example, TAs should have punctual attendance to their duties, 
complete the marking of assignments or lab reports within one week after 
due dates, and provide feedback to students in a timely manner. 
2.4 Individuals applying for a TA position must first obtain the consent of 
their supervisor or direct reporting officer, while the applicants should 
ensure that their TA duties will not adversely affect their normal academic 
and research performance. 
2.5 TAs should maintain detailed records of their work, including weekly 
TA work logs (Appendix 1), mid-term work report (Appendix 2), and end-
of-term work report (Appendix 3). These logs and reports are important 
materials for evaluating TAs.

3. The Requirements and Responsibilities of Course Instructors 
in Managing Teaching Assistant 
3.1 The course instructor is responsible for managing TAs of his/her course, 
guiding, and training them in their daily work, supervising the quality of 
TA work and attitude of TAs, and promptly improving the TAs' work 
outcomes. For example, before the semester begins, the instructor should 
clearly inform the TAs of their job responsibilities and stipend amounts. 
During the semester, the instructor should provide timely guidance to the 
TAs, keep track of TA work performance that includes but is not limited 
to answering students' questions, monitoring student assignments, 
gathering student feedback after lecture and in tutorial sessions. 
Furthermore, instructors should complete mid-term and end-of-term 
assessments of their TAs on time. 
3.2 The course instructor should make sure that TAs complete their work 
on time and with high quality. If it is found that a teaching assistant is 
unable to fulfill or complete the TA duties, the instructor should promptly 
provide guidance, urge TA to improve the work quality and attitude. If the 
teaching assistant's performance does not improve thereafter, the course 
instructor should promptly inform the relevant academic affairs office. 
Upon investigation by ZJUI, actions can be taken in accordance with the 
provisions in article 6.4. 
4. Assignment of Teaching Assistant Position and Quota 

- 4 - 
 
4.1 In accordance with the needs of course, ZJUI provides high priority to 
assign TA positions in the core technical courses of undergraduate and 
graduate curriculum, especially the courses with large enrollment from 
several academic programs. 
4.2 ZJUI sets up teaching assistant positions based on the nature of the 
course: 
4.2.1. According to the following method, TA quota for each course is 
proposed: 
i) For a course with 3~4 AUs, teaching weeks are ≥ 14 weeks in one 
semester, and enrollment ≥ 60 students, 1 TA quota is assigned in principle 
for the enrollment of every 60 students. 
ii) For a course with large enrollment from multiple programs, in principle: 
- 0.5 TA quota will be assigned to each parallel discussion/tutorial/lab 
session (those instructed by lab staffs are excluded). 
- Every 60-students enrollment can have 0.5 quota for grading TA if 
necessary. 
4.2.2 Academic Affairs Office for Undergraduates or Academic Affairs 
Office for Graduates would adjust TA quota of the courses according to 
the nature of the courses, such as the type of course, the number of teaching 
hours, the number of students enrolled, the demands for TA work, and the 
previous implementation, etc. 
4.3 A TA position that meets all the following requirements is in principle 

- 5 - 
 
considered as a full-time TA quota. 
4.3.1 TA fulfill the full duties for more than 14 weeks in one semester. 
4.3.2 TA works at least 10 hours per week. 
4.3.3 If there are any special circumstances, Academic Affairs Office for 
Undergraduates or Academic Affairs Office for Graduates will determine 
the workload. 
4.3.4 TA receive scores as “Pass” or above in both TA evaluations of the 
semester. 
5. Application, Appointment and Training of Teaching Assistants 
5.1 TA positions are set up and appointed according to the following 
process: 
5.1.1 The course instructor submits an application for TA position to the 
relevant academic affairs office at the end of the semester preceding to the 
one the course is offered. Based on the nature of the course and the actual 
needs, the instructor shall provide detailed information on the TA's 
responsibilities and workload requirements via Appendix 4. Academic 
Affairs Office for Undergraduates or Graduates will confirm the TA quota 
according to the nature of the course and the previous implementation. 
5.1.2 According to the TA quota approved, the TA recruitment information 
will be posted to the academic affairs website of ZJUI. Candidates can only 
apply through this online platform for TA positions. The applications will 
be forwarded to course instructor for review. Once selected by instructor, 

- 6 - 
 
the application will then be forwarded to the relevant academic affairs 
offices for final approval. Applications that did not apply through the ZJUI 
website or were not approved by ZJUI, are considered invalid, and the 
candidate will not be recognized as a TA and will not receive TA 
allowances. 
5.2 In principle, each candidate can only apply for one TA position. Priority 
is given to graduate students and above. Under any circumstances, 
undergraduate students should not be appointed as head TA.  
5.2.1 Sophomore and above in full-time program, postgraduate students, 
postdoc research fellows or research associates can be appointed to TA 
positions of undergraduate courses. 
5.2.2. In principle, TAs of postgraduate courses are selected from the 
current graduate students of ZJUI. In special circumstances, ZJU 
employees with master’s degree and above or middle-level certificates can 
be appointed to TA position of postgraduate courses. 
5.3 All of the candidates that apply for TA positions of ZJUI should meet 
the following criteria: 
(1)  Firm belief in and adherence to the core socialist values; 
(2) Good conduct, physically and mentally healthy, with a sense of 
responsibility and initiative; 
(3)  Good academic performance, having previously taken the course for 
which they are applying to be a teaching assistant and received an A grade 

- 7 - 
 
or above, and possessing certain level of organizing, managing, and 
communication skills. 
(4)  Has good spoken and written English level and can handle professional 
communications related to the course contents in English. 
(5)  Has no record of violating school regulations or engaging in academic 
misconducts. 
6. TA Evaluations 
6.1 The relevant academic affairs offices of ZJUI are responsible for 
formulating the relevant requirements for TAs and the management for TA 
in accordance with the objectives of student cultivation, the characteristics 
of the major, and the actual situation of education programs at ZJUI. 
According to this, the office carries out the establishment of TA positions, 
the selection and appointment of TAs, and the disbursement of allowances. 
ZJUI should designate specific staff to be responsible for TA management 
matters and establish the records of TAs. 
6.2 All of the TAs are required to participate in a training session for TAs 
that is organized by the relevant academic affairs offices at the beginning 
of each semester in order to acquire the basic knowledge and skills of TAs. 
Absent from the training session without a valid reason will be taken into 
account in the midterm evaluation of the TA. 
6.3 The evaluations of TAs are organized twice per semester by the 
relevant academic affairs offices. The first is the midterm evaluation 

- 8 - 
 
(conducted in the seventh week of teaching), and the second is the end-of-
term evaluation (conducted in the fourteenth week of teaching). The 
midterm evaluation is mainly conducted by the course instructor, and the 
evaluation outcome is either “Pass” or “Fail”. TA who receives a score of 
“Fail” in midterm evaluation will be handled according to the provisions 
of Article 6.4.  
The end-of-term evaluation combines the evaluation outcome from the 
students and the course instructors. The evaluation scores from students 
and the instructor account for 50% of the total evaluation, respectively. The 
evaluation outcome of the TAs is categorized into three levels: Excellent, 
Pass, and Fail. The proportion of excellent TAs should not exceed 20%. 
Teaching assistant who receives a score of “Fail” in end-of-term evaluation, 
will be handled according to the provisions of Article 6.4. 
6.4 For TAs who are unable to continue working or are unable to perform 
their duties, ZJUI may remove them from TA positions. For TAs who do 
not fulfill their job responsibilities, ZJUI may terminate their TA work at 
any time and stop their TA allowance. If a TA receives a “Fail” in any TA 
evaluations, ZJUI may terminate the TA work, remove him/her from TA 
position and stop the TA allowance immediately. 
7. Allowance of Teaching Assistants 
7.1 ZJUI provides an allowance to TAs in accordance with the actual 
workload. The allowance standard is 5000 yuan per semester (four months) 

- 9 - 
 
for one TA quota. 
7.2 The allowance is paid to TAs once every 2 months. TAs must 
participate in the midterm and end-of-term evaluations organized by the 
relevant academic affairs offices and achieve a score as Pass or higher in 
order to receive the allowance. Within 4 working days after the completion 
of the TA evaluation, the relevant academic affairs office will submit the 
list of TAs who are eligible for the allowance and the amount of the 
allowance to the director of the relevant academic affairs offices for review, 
and then to ZJUI's General Administration office. The relevant academic 
affairs offices must ensure the accuracy and completeness of the list, and 
the list must be confirmed and signed by the director of the relevant 
academic affairs offices. The General Administration office of ZJUI must 
disburse the allowance to the students within one week after the office 
receives the list. 
8. Appendix 
This regulation shall come into force upon its official publication by ZJUI. 
The interpretation of this document lies to the Academic Affairs Office for 
Undergraduates, Academic Affairs Office for Graduates, and General 
Administration Office of ZJUI.
