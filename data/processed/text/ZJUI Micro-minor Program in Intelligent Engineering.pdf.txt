ZJUI Micro-minor Program in Intelligent
Engineering
In a world driven by rapid technological advancement, interdisciplinary
collaboration, and dynamic problem-solving, Zhejiang University / University of
Illinois Urbana-Champaign Institute (ZJUI) proudly introduce the Micro-minor
Program in Intelligent Engineering (MPIE). This transformative education
program, approved by Zhejiang University, is designed to prepare students for a
future where intelligence systems are at the heart of global engineering
challenges.
 
1.Overview
 
As a globally-oriented institute of higher education, ZJUI is committed to
fostering interdisciplinary learning and innovative skill development. The MPIE is
a forward-thinking initiative aimed at broadening the educational horizons of our
undergraduate programs in Civil Engineering, Computer Engineering, Electrical
Engineering and Mechanical Engineering.
 
MPIE has four focus areas that address complex engineering challenges:
 
Robotics and Automation: This area covers advanced concepts in robotic
systems, autonomous operation, and smart manufacturing, preparing
students to excel in sectors such as industrial automation, healthcare, and
service industries.

2. Requirements
 
To complete a MPIE program, students must fulfill the following:
 
1.1 Coursework (12 credits)
 
Students must complete a minimum of 12 additional credits in interdisciplinary
engineering courses. These credits, detailed in Section 4 of the program
curriculum, cannot be double-counted toward the requirements of both the
student's primary and MPIE programs. However, up to 6 free elective credits may
be applied.
 
1.2 Multi-disciplinary Senior Design Project or Thesis
 
Students are required to complete a senior design project or thesis that integrates
principles across their primary program with MPIE focus areas. The design courses
ECE 445, ME 470 or ST 4001 may fulfill this requirement alongside primary
graduation requirements.
 
Upon completion of these requirements, students will be awarded a certificate
from Zhejiang University, signifying their proficiency in a MPIE area and their
ability to apply core principles to solve complex problems. This certificate will also
serve as evidence of their dedication and commitment to a rigorous and
challenging program of study and will enhance their career prospects in the
engineering industry.

3. Application
 
3.1 Eligibility
 
GPA Requirement: Students must maintain a minimum cumulative GPA of 3.0
in both their major-specific courses and their overall coursework.
 
Coursework: Eligible students should have completed specific courses from
MPIE curriculum and are expected to fulfill all requirements by the end of the
spring semester of their senior year.
 
3.2 Registration
 
Students interested in a MPIE program must submit an application form through
the application portal during the Spring semester of their senior year. The
application should include:
 
A summary of relevant courses completed,
A description of how their senior design project aligns with a MPIE focus
area,
A brief personal statement outlining their interest in the program.
 
3.3 Certification
 
The admission committee will assess applications based on academic
performance, commitment to MPIE, relevant coursework and senior design or
thesis project. Notifications regarding admission decisions will be sent out after
evaluations are complete. Each student can only be admitted to one MPIE focus
area. Qualified students will receive a micro-minor certificate of “Intelligent
Engineering” in their chosen focus area from Zhejiang University, recognizing
their interdisciplinary skills and expertise.
 
4. Curriculum
 
To fulfill the MPIE curriculum requirements, students may take advantage of
course resources not only at ZJUI but also during exchanges at UIUC or at
Zhejiang University’s main campus. Additional courses not listed in the curriculum
but deemed equivalent in content may be approved by ZJUI for credit toward the
program requirements.

4.2 Artificial Intelligence
 
The Artificial Intelligence (AI) area focuses on the creation and application of
computer systems capable of performing tasks traditionally requiring human
intelligence, such as image recognition, natural language processing, and
complex decision-making. This interdisciplinary field integrates principles from
computer science, mathematics, and statistics to equip students with skills in
machine learning, neural networks, and AI ethics, preparing them to contribute to
rapidly evolving AI applications across industries like healthcare, finance, and
technology.

4.3 Data Science and Analytics
 
The Data Science and Analytics area focuses on employing statistical and
computational methods to derive valuable insights from large, complex datasets.
This interdisciplinary field merges principles from statistics, computer science, and
engineering, equipping students with skills in data mining, predictive modeling,
and data visualization. Students in this area are prepared to address data-driven
challenges across sectors, including healthcare, finance, social media, and beyond,
where informed decision-making is essential.

4.4 Smart Cities
 
The Smart Cities area centers on using technology and data to improve urban
living by enhancing infrastructure, optimizing services, and minimizing
environmental impact. This interdisciplinary field brings together principles from
civil engineering, electrical engineering, and computer science, enabling students
to work on sustainable urban development projects. Key topics include Internet of
Things (IoT) integration, smart infrastructure, energy management, and
environmental monitoring, all aimed at creating efficient, resilient, and livable
cities for the future.
